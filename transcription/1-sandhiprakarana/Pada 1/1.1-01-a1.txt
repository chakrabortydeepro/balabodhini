                    <l xml:id="BBP-1.1.1.1.2" style="stream2"><label>BBP

                        <caesura/>

                    <l xml:id="BBP-1.1.1.1.3" style="stream2"><label>BBP 1.1.1.1.3</label>yas

                        <caesura/>

                        <caesura/>

                        <caesura/>

                    <l xml:id="BBP-1.1.1.1.4" style="stream2"><label>BBP 1.1.1.1.4</label>tatputraḥ

                    <l xml:id="BBP-1.1.1.1.6" style="stream2"><label>BBP

                    <ab corresp="#BB-1.1.6-2" type="apparatus">
                        <list type="translation">
                            <item corresp="#BB-1.1.6-2-t">The purpose of considering the diphthongs
                                as long: <foreign>ro re lopaṃ svaraś ca pūrvo dīrghaḥ
                                    (1.5.17)</foreign> (<foreign>r</foreign> followed by
                                    <foreign>r</foreign> undergoes deletion and the preceding vowel
                                is lengthened). [E.g.] <foreign>rave raśmiḥ</foreign> (the ray of
                                the sun), <foreign>svai rakṣā</foreign> (protection by themselves),
                                    <foreign>go rajjuḥ</foreign> (cow's tether), <foreign>dyau
                                    ramyā</foreign> (charming heaven). Here, according to the rule
                                    <foreign>nāmiparo ram (1.5.12)</foreign> (<foreign>ḥ</foreign>
                                preceded by a <foreign>nāmin</foreign> turns into
                                    <foreign>r</foreign>), <foreign>r</foreign> occurs. Upon its
                                deletion, since definitions apply like rainfall (i.e. applies
                                universally), the sounds <foreign>e</foreign> and so on (the
                                diphthongs) themselves appear at the place of the sounds
                                    <foreign>e</foreign> and so on (i.e. they remain the same). For,
                                if the diphthongs were not long, just as they become short in words
                                like <foreign>atiri</foreign> (<foreign>&#60;ati-rai</foreign>
                                'extravagant') or <foreign>atinu</foreign>
                                    (<foreign>&#60;ati-nau</foreign> 'surpassing a ship'), there
                                would have been long <foreign>ī</foreign> and <foreign>ū</foreign>
                                (substitutions) in place of <foreign>e</foreign> or
                                    <foreign>ai</foreign> and <foreign>o</foreign> or
                                    <foreign>au</foreign>, respectively, as per homogeneity in terms
                                of place.</item>
                        </list>
                    </ab>

                        vācyam | bhūyasā vyapadeśā bhavantīti tālavyauṣṭhyabhāgayor bhūyastvāt
                        tālavyauṣṭhyāv īdūtāv eva syātām | adhyardhamātrā tālavyā edaitoḥ, oṣṭhyā
                        odautoḥ, ardhamātraiva kaṇṭhyeti śikṣāvidaḥ |</p>

                    <ab corresp="#BB-1.1.6-3" type="apparatus">
                        <list type="translation">
                            <item corresp="#BB-1.1.6-3-t">In the same way as the palate and lips,
                                the throat also serves as the place of articulation for diphthongs.
                                But considering this, one should not say that there can be
                                    <foreign>ā</foreign> (as a replacement for the diphthongs) as
                                well as it falls into the same class (having throat as the place of
                                articulation). Representation is determined by what is larger, and
                                since the palatal and the labial portions are larger, only
                                    <foreign>ī</foreign> and <foreign>ū</foreign>, that are palatal
                                and labial respectively, are permissible. According to phoneticians,
                                one and a half moras of both <foreign>e</foreign> and
                                    <foreign>ai</foreign> are palatal, while for
                                    <foreign>o</foreign> and <foreign>au</foreign>, they are labial,
                                and only half a mora (of the diphthongs) is guttural. </item>
                        </list>
                    </ab>
